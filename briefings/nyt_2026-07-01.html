Dozens of U.S. Deportees Killed or Missing After Venezuela Earthquakes

World

Twin earthquakes devastated Venezuela with an official death toll of 1,719, though experts fear a serious undercount. Among the dead and missing are dozens of people deported from the U.S. only hours earlier, some killed at the airport and others in collapsed buildings in Caracas and coastal La Guaira. USMCA Trade Agreement Faces Critical Review, Renegotiation Looms

Business / Economy

July 1 triggered the USMCA's mandated six-year review, which could extend the pact for another 16 years if all three countries agree. The Trump administration has signaled objections and is expected to demand stricter auto rules of origin, new limits on Chinese-owned factories in Mexico, and easier U.S. tariff authority. With nearly $1.5 trillion in annual trilateral trade, business groups warn that collapse would cause economic chaos.

▶ Cluster 3: Supreme Court, Law & Civil Liberties

Supreme Court Delivers Enduring Conservative Wins Despite Losses for Trump

Politics

The Supreme Court wrapped up a consequential term with major conservative victories alongside a headline liberal win on birthright citizenship. The court struck down limits on coordinated party spending (6–3), allowed states to ban transgender athletes from girls' sports (6–3), and expanded presidential power to fire independent agency officials. On birthright citizenship, the court voted 5–4 to block Trump's executive order, with Chief Justice Roberts joining the four liberal justices.

Birthright Citizenship Decision Surprised Legal Scholars With Its Closeness

Politics

The Supreme Court's 5–4 ruling reaffirmed that birthright citizenship is guaranteed by the 14th Amendment's Citizenship Clause. Chief Justice Roberts wrote that the clause "unambiguously grants citizenship to any child born in the United States, regardless of their parents' immigration status." Legal experts called the narrow margin shocking given that birthright citizenship has been settled law for more than a century. The four dissenters argued the 14th Amendment's original meaning did not require citizenship for children of undocumented immigrants.

Three Legal Scholars Assess a Tumultuous Supreme Court Term

Opinion

Kate Shaw, William Baude, and Stephen I. Vladeck offered assessments of the term. Shaw found the birthright decision the least surprising but called the 5–4 split worrisome. Baude said the court got the right result but used thin reasoning that will invite further litigation. Vladeck called the campaign finance ruling in F.E.C. v. Ted Cruz for Senate "a dramatic expansion of money in politics" that will fundamentally change how campaigns are financed.

Professor Fired for Criticizing Charlie Kirk Reaches $1.9 Million Settlement

U.S.

Tamar Shirinian, a former assistant professor of anthropology at the University of Tennessee at Knoxville, reached a $1.9 million settlement after being fired in 2024 for a social media post calling conservative activist Charlie Kirk "a fascist." Shirinian sued, arguing her First Amendment rights were violated and that the university bowed to political pressure. Free-speech advocates said the settlement sends a clear message that public universities cannot fire professors simply because their political speech displeases powerful figures.

New York

New York City is preparing for triple-digit temperatures expected to hit 102 degrees by Thursday. Mayor Zohran Mamdani is applying lessons from a winter blizzard that killed more than two dozen people, activating about 600 cooling centers and launching 15 outreach vans staffed by nurses to provide wellness checks and emergency transportation. A later February storm that used the new protocols recorded zero deaths.

Medicare Expands GLP-1 Drug Coverage for Older Adults, but Risks Require Caution

Well / Health

A Medicare pilot program, the Medicare Obesity Treatment Demonstration, began Wednesday, covering GLP-1 drugs like Wegovy and Zepbound for beneficiaries with a BMI of 30 or higher. Up to 7 million beneficiaries could be eligible. Previously, Medicare Part D was prohibited by law from covering weight-loss medications. Experts caution that the risk-benefit calculation differs for older adults, with potential gastrointestinal side effects, muscle loss, and drug interactions. The pilot requires participation in a counseling program.
