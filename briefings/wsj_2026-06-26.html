Geopolitics & Defense

Iran and the Strait of Hormuz: Fragile Truce Under Fire

The Persian Gulf remains the briefing's center of gravity. A detailed account published this week describes how Iranian strikes devastated the U.S. naval base in Bahrain, forcing a fundamental recalculation of American force posture. Against that backdrop, an Iranian attack on a cargo ship in the Strait of Hormuz tests the durability of President Trump's deal to reopen the strategic waterway.

The attack signals that Iran intends to remain confrontational rather than settling into the deal's cooperative framework.

Calibrated view: 35% probability of another Iranian attack on commercial shipping within 30 days. The cargo-ship strike reads as a calibrated probe — Iran is testing enforcement thresholds without triggering full abrogation — but the strategic incentive to maximize negotiating leverage before the deal's implementation phases suggests further provocations are more likely than not within a quarter.

An exclusive WSJ report details Iran's estimate that it could reap $40 billion in fees from reopening the Strait in partnership with Gulf states — a calculation that frames Iran as positioning itself as the gatekeeper demanding tribute rather than an equal partner. In parallel, Iran is using China's yuan to conduct international transactions, a practice (reported by Rory Jones, Austin Ramzy, and Costas Paris) that circumvents U.S. dollar dominance and has expanded significantly since the Hormuz reopening talks began. This de-dollarization campaign represents a structural, long-game assault on American financial hegemony.

Cross-reference: Oil's retreat to $69.49/barrel — covered in Cluster 2 — directly pressures Iran's $40 billion windfall calculation and reflects the market's tentative return to prewar normal. The cargo-ship attack pulls that narrative back toward alarm.

Drone Warfare: The Ukraine Precedent Spreads to Asia

Ukraine's rapidly expanding drone fleet is overwhelming Russian air defense systems, shifting the tactical balance on the battlefield. Ukraine's drone strikes on Russian oil refineries are producing observable domestic effects, including fuel shortages and lines at gas stations — evidence that Russia's distribution network is under genuine strain.

South Korea has announced a sweeping military modernization plan requiring all active-duty soldiers to be trained in drone operations — a direct institutional absorption of lessons from the Ukraine war. This is a deliberate capacity build for future threats, not a reactive measure.

China's Sanctions Toolkit

China is developing a more sophisticated arsenal for retaliating against foreign sanctions, including expanded export controls and financial countermeasures. This sharpening of tools — combined with Iran's yuan-based de-dollarization — creates a parallel financial ecosystem that complicates Washington's leverage.

Economy & Markets

Tech Selloff Intensifies

Nasdaq futures fell sharply heading into Friday (June 26), extending a multi-day rout that has gripped the technology sector. S&P 500 futures also declined. The VIX rose above 20 — the threshold where markets shift from risk-taking to protective selling. Apple, Microsoft, and other tech giants saw significant pre-market declines. The selloff is driven by valuation concerns and macroeconomic headwinds, with the market collective doubting whether the AI capex cycle will deliver returns commensurate with spending.

Dan Gallagher, writing in Heard on the Street, notes that Micron's guidance makes clear the memory chip shortage will last — but that memory customers are developing workarounds through innovation in chip architecture and alternative technologies, posing a long-term threat to the boom cycle. This tension between current shortage and future substitution is the structural contradiction at the heart of the semiconductor trade. Apple's price increases on Macs and iPads (by $200 or more on select models) and Microsoft's Xbox price increases, both citing rising component costs, demonstrate that the shortage is passing through to consumers.

Calibrated view: 55% probability the Nasdaq correction deepens another 5%+ within two weeks. VIX above 20, unresolved valuation concerns all argue for continued pressure. However, the strategic discipline of institutional investors — sitting on hands, waiting for data — may limit the rout's velocity.

2026年6月26日 — 美元环流的压力测试

如果你把今天的市场看作一个系统，它的核心问题是：美元环流正在逆转，而系统内的每一个阀门都在承受压力。

先看最显性的信号。油价从战时峰值暴跌至69.49美元，VIX突破20，纳斯达克深度回调。这不是一个孤立的科技股调整——它是一个反馈回路的启动：AI泡沫的估值逻辑正在被利率成本和需求衰减双双挤压。半导体短缺传导至消费端（苹果Mac/iPad涨价200美元），而替代技术正在芯片架构层面瓦解这个短缺叙事。这是典型的周期顶部矛盾——供给紧张和需求破坏同时发生，而市场选择了向下交易。

伊朗的动向是另一个维度。40亿美元霍尔木兹过路费，本质上是一种地缘租金，但它的兑现前提是油价稳定在70美元之上。原油每跌一美元，德黑兰的谈判筹码就少一分。更值得关注的是，伊朗正在用人民币进行国际结算——这是一个平行金融系统的压力测试。当石油交易脱离CHIPS和SWIFT，美元环流的中游就出现了一条旁路。这不是试探，这是结构性去美元化的加速阀门。

日元已经承受着161+的压力，结构性利差让日本央行骑虎难下。日本在一个月内抛售470亿美元美债的场景并非历史——它是已经在系统中编码的故障路径。一旦日元触发更大规模的储备资产回流，美债收益率曲线的反馈回路将进一步收紧全球美元流动性。

华盛顿的预算龙头再次大开——Paletta的报道证实了系统性财政扩张的惯性。这恰恰是美元环流悖论的核心：美国需要财政赤字来维持需求，但持续的赤字会加速全球去美元化的政治意愿。JPMorgan接班人的争夺（Rohrbaugh vs. Petno）本身就是一个信号——当华尔街最大银行的后台从商业银行家转为衍生品交易员，说明金融高边疆的作战方式正在改变。

胡雪岩倒台的时候，没有人意识到是银路断了。今天，我们看到的每一个信号——去美元化、AI估值收缩、日元压力、油价震荡——都是同一个系统的不同故障表象。物以稀为贵，而此刻最稀缺的，是美元环流的信用共识。
