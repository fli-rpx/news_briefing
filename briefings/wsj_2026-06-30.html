Yen at 40-Year Low: Carry Trade at Maximum Extension

The Japanese yen weakened to a 40-year low against the U.S. dollar, "putting traders on alert for potential intervention by Japanese authorities" (WSJ Homepage, "Yen Falls to 40-Year Low Versus Dollar, Traders Alert for Potential FX Intervention"). At an estimated ¥165+ per dollar, the currency has breached levels that in prior cycles (e.g., ¥152 in 2022 and 2024) triggered intervention by the Bank of Japan and finance ministry — a historical pattern worth monitoring. The yen carry trade, which has funded a significant portion of leveraged equity longs, represents a systemic vulnerability: a snapback on intervention would force a multi-asset unwind. Comcast Splits: The Unbundling of Media

Comcast announced plans to split its business, spinning off NBCUniversal and Sky cable networks into a separate publicly traded company as "competition from streaming services escalates" (WSJ Homepage and Business, "Comcast Plans Company Split as Competition Escalates"). The vertical-integration thesis that drove Comcast's acquisition of NBCUniversal a decade ago is being unwound, reflecting how "the case for bundling media with distribution has weakened in the streaming era," as the WSJ's Heard on the Street column notes. The spinoff makes the standalone networks more exposed to cord-cutting pressures without Comcast's distribution cash-flow cross-subsidy. (→ *cross-ref: Stocks lift on Comcast news, Markets cluster*)
