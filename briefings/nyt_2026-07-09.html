Collapse of the U.S.-Iran Cease-Fire

The three-week-old U.S.-Iran cease-fire has unraveled dramatically. The U.S. military struck Iranian targets for a second consecutive night (July 8-9), with Iran retaliating by firing missiles and drones at U.S. bases in Kuwait and Bahrain. President Trump declared at the NATO summit in Turkey, "I think it's over," preemptively ending any pretense of diplomatic resolution. The U.S. Treasury had rescinded an Iranian oil sanctions waiver on July 7, and tanker attacks in the Strait of Hormuz this week signaled the truce's fragility. The International Maritime Organization has urged ships to avoid the strait, threatening to reverse the modest recovery in shipping traffic. Brent crude oil trades at about $77/barrel, down 1.5% on the day but above pre-war levels of $70. 这不是油价涨跌的问题，这是美元环流正在遭遇制度熵增的问题。每次美国用制裁武器，被制裁国就加速去美元化。这次停火崩溃后，伊朗的石油客户会在人民币结算上走得更远。
