Jobs Report: 57,000 Added, Participation Rate at Lowest Since March 2021

The U.S. economy added 57,000 jobs in June — roughly one-third of the ~200K monthly spring average. The unemployment rate fell,

but only because the labor-force participation rate dropped to its lowest level since March 2021: more people left the workforce

entirely rather than finding jobs. The Data Analyst flags this as the single most overlooked number in the entire edition: "A falling

participation rate coupled with falling unemployment is a negative mix — it means the shrinking denominator is doing the work, not the

expanding numerator." The Data Analyst also notes the standard error on the Establishment Survey is roughly ±100K, meaning the

true 90% confidence interval could range from -50K to +160K. "Framing this single number as a definitive 'snap' of the hot streak is

narrative-building, not statistical inference." The Operator warns the Fed's rate tool manages demand, not labor supply: "People

leaving the workforce due to long COVID, caregiving burdens, opioid-related disability, or early retirement do not return when the Fed

cuts rates." *(Story #1)*
