Geopolitics & Defense

US-Iran Interim Peace Deal Signed at Versailles

President Trump signed an interim peace agreement with Iran at the Chateau de Versailles, flanked by French President Emmanuel Macron. The deal sidesteps key reasons the U.S. went to war with Iran — which began after Iran launched a missile barrage at Israel in 2024 following Israel's killing of Hezbollah leader Hassan Nasrallah.

Vice President JD Vance has emerged as the deal's primary defender, arguing it is a "win for the U.S. no matter what happens next." Vance postponed travel to Switzerland for talks with Tehran. He acknowledged that "not every detail has been put in writing."

Calibrated view: 55% probability the interim deal holds through the full 60-day negotiation period — Vance's political investment and economic relief (falling gas prices, Hormuz reopening) create incentives for both sides, but Israel-Hezbollah clashes and Tehran hardliners pose material risks.

Israel-Hezbollah Clashes Test Deal's Durability

Four Israeli soldiers and at least 18 people in Lebanon were killed in renewed fighting. The violence underscores the deal's fragility — Hezbollah operates independently of Tehran's diplomatic calculus.

Vance Slams Israeli Critics; Hegseth Threatens NATO Cuts

Vice President Vance took a combative stance against Israeli critics, with WSJ's politics desk noting he "now owns the agreement as talks enter a difficult new phase." Separately, Defense Secretary Hegseth announced a review of U.S. forces in Europe, threatening NATO funding cuts if European members don't increase defense spending.

Military officials told lawmakers the Pentagon needs $80 billion for the Iran war and other expenses — the services could run out of money this summer without a new wartime spending bill.

Calibrated view: 40% probability of significant U.S. troop reduction in Europe within 12 months — Hegseth's review provides the mechanism, and the $80B funding gap strengthens the case, but NATO allies will resist.

翟东升——体系/结构视角

伊朗停火协议在凡尔赛宫签署，这不仅是战场停火，更是全球货币体系的一次结构性重组信号。石油美元环流的核心环节——霍尔木兹海峡——重新开放，意味着美元回流机制获得喘息。但美联储的鹰派转向（9/19官员勾画加息路径）揭示了更深层的矛盾：美国需要弱势美元来维系出口竞争力和债务可持续性，却因国内通胀压力被迫接受强势美元。这个悖论无解。

中国对欧出口洪流正在测试欧洲的贸易战承受力。欧洲夹在中美之间，既想对中国商品设限，又担心美国同时加压（药价贸易调查、北约军费要求）。这是典型的"平行体系"压力测试——当美国主导的旧体系无法容纳中国的产能过剩，区域化、阵营化的新体系就会加速形成。

Warsh的Fed工作组（沟通、资产负债表、通胀框架）本质上是美元信用体系的制度修缮。但修缮解决不了结构性问题：当全球贸易从美元单一体系向多元化储备体系演变时，任何Fed改革都只是延缓而非阻止这一进程。
