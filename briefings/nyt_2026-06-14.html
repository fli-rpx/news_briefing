Global Signal: The 60-Day Pause

A System Under Stress — War oscillates, alliances fracture, feedback loops break.

Executive Summary

On June 14, 2026, the United States and Iran signed a 60-day ceasefire deal reopening the Strait of Hormuz while deferring nuclear talks — a deal far short of Trump's "unconditional surrender" demand. Israel erupted in bipartisan rage at being sold out. Trump turned 80, surrounded by an MMA octagon and declining public confidence — nearly 6 in 10 Americans see him as "more erratic." His embrace of China as a "G2" peer is upending alliances across Asia and Europe simultaneously: the G7 convenes in Évian with European leaders no longer viewing the US as a partner but "in some cases, as a threat." Germany and Japan are rearming with "justifiable fear that the United States might sell them out." British forces seized a Russian shadow fleet tanker in the English Channel. Belfast burned in anti-immigrant riots. The Knicks won their first NBA championship in 53 years, producing New York's first collective joy unmoored from tragedy. SpaceX hit a $2 trillion valuation. The aggregate sentiment is decisively negative (−0.50 mean, 54.5% negative/very negative), with only 13.6% positive.
