Iran-U.S. Conflict Escalates Over Strait of Hormuz

The June memorandum of understanding between President Trump and Iran has collapsed. Iran interpreted the agreement as giving it control over the Strait of Hormuz; when challenged, the MOU offered no institutional recourse beyond escalation. The Iranian military fired at U.S. targets across the Persian Gulf, and the U.S. military responded by hitting dozens of Iranian targets. Iran's military said it launched more drones at American military sites in the region. President Trump denied Iran's claim to have closed the strait, through which approximately 20% of global oil supplies pass. Iranian officials, including Ayatollah Mohsen Araki, framed revenge as a "binding command" targeting Trump and Israeli Prime Minister Netanyahu.

Beijing launched a long-range intercontinental ballistic missile into the Pacific Ocean — a first for China, which had previously tested nuclear missiles only within its borders.
