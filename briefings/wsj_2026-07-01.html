WSJ DAILY BRIEFING

Wednesday, July 1, 2026

简报快照

The July 1 edition captures a market and geopolitical landscape at maximum structural tension. The S&P 500 just delivered its best quarter in six years (+15%), the Nasdaq surged 21%, and global equity fundraising hit $729.4B in H1 — the second-best start on record — all driven by an AI boom that has South Korea's semiconductor exports exploding 199.5% to $44.8B. Yet beneath the euphoria, the machinery is straining: stocks slipped today as Fed Chairman Kevin Warsh dodged questions on July rate decisions, the yen hit a 40-year low at 162.69, gold fell below $4,000 on rate-hike fears, the U.S.-Iran talks face internal Iranian divisions, Saudi Arabia openly blocked a U.S. military operation in the Strait of Hormuz, and the Supreme Court delivered a 6-3 rebuke to President Trump on birthright citizenship. The dominant risk being systematically underpriced: the leverage that finances the AI rally remains vulnerable to a single volatility event — whether from Tokyo, Tehran, or a margin desk.

Cluster 1: 地缘政治与国防

Iran Talks Are a Holding Action Under Internal Fracture

Three stories in today's WSJ paint a picture of diplomatic fragility compounded by Iranian internal divisions and alliance stress. Iran's civilian leaders are "seeking billions in frozen assets" while hardline military officials aim to control the Strait of Hormuz (WSJ World, "Iran's Diverging Priorities Are Jeopardizing U.S. Peace Talks"). Trump was briefed on all-out war options but told aides he is fine continuing negotiations past an Aug. 18 deadline (WSJ World Exclusive, "Trump Briefed on All-Out War Options in Iran but Opts to Stick With Talks"). The U.S. and Iran are over a week into 60 days of talks, with key contentions including Strait of Hormuz fees and nuclear work restrictions.

The U.S.-Saudi Rift Is the Day's Most Underreported Geopolitical Development

Saudi Arabia blocked Trump's first big effort to open the Strait of Hormuz — Operation "Project Freedom" — as more than 100 U.S. military aircraft were taking off (WSJ World Exclusive, "A Dispute Over Opening Hormuz Drives a Wedge Into U.S.-Saudi Relations"). The U.S. is now "considering reducing its military footprint in the kingdom." This represents a fundamental fracture in a half-century alliance. For investors, this introduces a risk premium on Gulf energy infrastructure that current oil prices have not absorbed. (→ *cross-ref: oil markets in Cluster 2*)

U.K. Rearms Amid Global Uncertainty

The U.K. pledged nearly $20 billion in additional defense spending amid global security concerns (WSJ World further reading), signaling that NATO allies are rearming in response to the deteriorating security environment, even as the U.S.-Saudi alliance frays.

Cluster 2: 市场与宏观经济

Stocks Slip as Warsh Dodges Rate Questions

U.S. stocks slipped on the first trading day of H2 2026 as investors scrutinized Fed Chairman Kevin Warsh's comments during a panel with the heads of the ECB, the Bank of England, and the Bank of Canada (WSJ Homepage, "Stock Market Today: Nasdaq Opens Lower, Warsh Dodges Questions on July Rate Decision"). Treasury yields were little changed after rising on strong job-opening numbers (WSJ Markets, "U.S. Stock Futures Slip as Investors Await Warsh Comments").

Wall Street's Best Quarter in Six Years — But Now What?

The S&P 500 rose 15% and the Nasdaq jumped 21% in Q2, the biggest quarterly rallies since Q2 2020, driven by the AI boom (WSJ Homepage, "Wall Street Just Had Its Best Quarter in Six Years. What's Next?"). Global equity fundraising topped $729.4B in H1 2026, boosted by SpaceX's record IPO, marking the second-best start on record (WSJ Homepage, "Tech Equity Raises Are Going Supersize"). The WSJ itself flags: "Historical peaks in tech fundraising offer reasons to be cautious." (→ *cross-ref: AI-driven export boom in Cluster 3*)

The Yen Crisis: 40-Year Low at 162.69

USD/JPY touched 162.69 — a fresh 40-year high, the highest intraday level since 1986 (WSJ World, "Yen Weakens Vs. Dollar; Market Remains on Alert for Possible FX Intervention"). Japanese Finance Minister Katayama said "we stand ready to take appropriate action whenever necessary." The MOF intervened at ~152 in both 2022 and 2024; current levels are well past that threshold. The critical linkage markets are ignoring: the yen carry trade funds leveraged positions in U.S. tech equities, creating the clearest contagion path — yen intervention → carry trade unwind → margin call cascade. (→ *cross-ref: AI rally dependency in Cluster 3*)

Gold Below $4,000 on Rate-Hike Fears

Gold crossed below $4,000 a troy ounce, falling as low as $3,973, heading for its worst quarter since 2013 (WSJ Markets, "Gold Back Below $4,000 on Rate-Hike Fears"). However, gold has doubled from ~$2,000 in early 2024, so the "decline" narrative requires context: it reflects a relative pullback from cycle highs, not a return to pre-bull levels.

Oil Markets Pricing Normality Into Abnormal Geopolitics

Brent crude held below $73 a barrel; WTI slipped 0.9% to $68.90 (WSJ Markets, "Oil Little Changed With Focus on U.S.-Iran Talks, Hormuz Recovery"). Oil markets are pricing in diplomatic resolution despite the U.S.-Saudi standoff and active Hormuz risk. If Hormuz faces disruption, prior base rates (1990 Gulf War, 2019 Abqaiq, 2023 Red Sea) suggest a $10-20/bbl spike.

Cluster 3: 商业与科技战略

The AI Boom Is Restructuring Everything — Markets, Trade, Employment

South Korea's exports jumped 79% in June — the fastest since October 1978 — with semiconductor exports surging 199.5% to $44.8B (WSJ Homepage, "South Korean Exports Soar on AI Chip Boom"). Global equity fundraising hit $729.4B in H1 (WSJ Homepage, "Tech Equity Raises Are Going Supersize"). (→ *cross-ref: Q2 rally in Cluster 2*)

The Other Side of AI: Structural Job Displacement

U.S. employers announced 45,849 job cuts in June, down 53% from 97,006 in May (WSJ Business, "U.S. Employer Layoff Plans Cool in June, Tech Leads Job Cuts"). AI was the leading reason cited, with 14,029 AI-related cuts. Andy Challenger: "AI is the dominant force as companies are restructuring around it." The 53% MoM decline exaggerates improvement against a pre-pandemic baseline (~40,000-60,000 monthly average), and AI-driven cuts appear structural rather than cyclical.

General Mills Swings to Loss

General Mills swung to a loss in its fiscal fourth quarter as it aims to "win back" price-sensitive consumers (WSJ Business, "General Mills Swings to Loss, Works to Win Back 'Challenging' Consumers"). Shares rose ~4% premarket despite the headline loss.

Nike's China Weakness

Nike reported a 1% revenue decline to $10.97B, but China segment sales dropped 12% (WSJ Business, "Nike's Sales Continue to Decline as China Weakness Persists"). CEO Elliott Hill: "We have to get back to growth in China. We have to be more premium and culturally relevant." The aggregate figure masks a regional collapse. The strong dollar makes U.S. goods more expensive in a market where domestic alternatives (Anta, Li-Ning) gain traction.

KKR and SK Launch $1.3B Renewable-Energy Platform

KKR and SK Inc. jointly launched a $1.3B renewable-energy platform for surging AI data-center and semiconductor manufacturing demand (WSJ Business, "KKR, SK to Jointly Launch $1.30 Billion Renewable-Energy Platform"). (→ *cross-ref: South Korea chip exports in Cluster 2*)

Anthropic Reaches Deal to Restore Fable AI Model

The Trump administration and Anthropic reached an agreement to restore the Fable AI model, shut down for 2½ weeks over security concerns (WSJ Markets, "Anthropic Reaches Deal With Trump Administration to Restore Access to Fable AI Model"). Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick confirmed the collaboration to review and authorize Fable 5. This sets a precedent: AI model access is now subject to executive branch review.

Cluster 4: 政策与观点

SCOTUS Rejects Birthright Citizenship Challenge (6-3)

The Supreme Court struck down President Trump's attempt to curtail birthright citizenship in a 6-3 decision (WSJ Homepage, "Supreme Court Rejects Trump's Bid to Curtail Birthright Citizenship"). Chief Justice Roberts wrote: "The Framers of the Fourteenth Amendment extended that promise to 'every free-born person in this land.' We keep that promise today." The WSJ Opinion Editorial Board frames this as "Trump Loses on Birthright Citizenship," calling the push "legal revisionism" (WSJ Opinion, "Trump Loses on Birthright Citizenship").

Trump's $1B+ Crypto Windfall

President Trump's latest financial disclosures show he earned more than a billion dollars last year on crypto deals — an unprecedented income surge for a sitting president (WSJ Homepage, "Trump Made More Than $1 Billion on Crypto Deals, Part of 2025 Windfall").

A Divided Country Nears July 4th

Jason Riley writes that America's milestone anniversaries coincide with periods of "industrial turbulence, political anxiety" — and this July 4th is no different (WSJ Opinion, "A Divided Country Celebrates Its Anniversary—Again"). The piece provides a philosophical counterpoint to the day's institutional stress signals.

Happy Days Again for TV News?

Holman W. Jenkins, Jr. examines whether TV news is seeing a resurgence; if streaming platforms give news a future, outlets "will want to be trusted again" (WSJ Opinion, "Happy Days Again for TV News?").

前景展望

The July 1 edition tells a single story through four lenses: Geopolitically, alliance structures are fracturing. Saudi blocked U.S. military access. Iran talks are internally divided. The U.K. is rearming. The assumption that Gulf allies are dependable partners is no longer operative. Markets are celebrating a historic Q2 but sliding into H2. The AI boom is real — S&P 500 +15%, Nasdaq +21%, South Korean chip exports +199.5% — but stocks slipped on the first day of the new quarter. Warsh's ambiguity, the yen at a 40-year low, and gold below $4,000 suggest the easy gains are behind us. Business is being restructured by AI in real time. The same technology driving record returns is driving 14,029 AI-related layoffs in June alone. Incumbent consumer brands (Nike, General Mills) face pressure while AI enablers boom. Capital flows into adjacent infrastructure (KKR/SK $1.3B renewable platform). Institutions are holding — but under asymmetric pressure. The Supreme Court upheld birthright citizenship (6-3), checking executive power. The Fed's independence is tested by a chair who declines to clarify policy. AI governance is being negotiated ad hoc between tech companies and the administration.

For the institutional reader: the surface says record quarter, AI exuberance, diplomatic negotiations. The subsurface says alliance fracture, currency crisis at a 40-year extreme, structural job displacement, and a market opening lower. The gap between these two narratives is where the next quarter's story will be written.

观察者评论

翟东升 — 主权信用与平行体系的临界点

今天的WSJ头条不是AI狂欢，不是历史性季度涨幅，而是沙特公开拒绝美军借道打通霍尔木兹海峡。这不是一次外交摩擦，这是美式全球化第三阶段进入结构性崩解的标志性事件。底层逻辑是什么？美国霸权的物质基础正在系统性钙化。当沙特——半个世纪以来美元-石油体系的基石盟友——对美军说"不"，这说明的不只是地区政策分歧，而是主权信用货币体系背后的契约执行能力正在瓦解。主权信用货币论告诉我们：美元的价值扎根于美国制度的可执行性和军事投射能力。当这两根柱子同时摇晃，无论美联储做什么，都治不了病根。再看日元跌至162.69，四十年新低。这不是货币政策问题，这是美元体系向日本转嫁通胀成本的必然结果。日本面临结构性两难：不干预则进口通胀撕裂国内经济，干预则抛售美债动摇自身最大海外资产。而沙特的反水、霍尔木兹的潜在封锁、以及全球AI产业链的剧烈重组（韩国芯片出口暴增199.5%背后是供应链加速向东亚集中），共同指向一个趋势：世界正在加速走向平行体系。美国主导的安全框架与金融框架不再能覆盖全球，大量国家将采取"双栖"策略——在安全上靠美国，在经济上靠中国；或者反之。这不是一两个季度的市场波动，这是国际秩序从单极向两极体系过渡的物理天花板正在迫近。

金灿荣 — 美国进入战略相持阶段

各位朋友，今天聊一聊7月1日的华尔街日报。你们看啊，这份报纸透出来的信息量非常大，讲的是一个事情——美国真的进入战略相持阶段了，而且有点吃力。先说最震撼的一条：沙特阿拉伯直接拒绝了美国军事行动，不让美军借道打通霍尔木兹海峡。一百多架美国飞机都起飞了，沙特说"不行"。这个事情其实很简单——你美国连自己养了七十年的小弟都指挥不动了，那你还是那个说一不二的霸主吗？过去沙特敢这样吗？不敢。为什么现在敢了？因为沙特看出来了，美国在中东的战略收缩是板上钉钉的事。再看美国国内。最高法院6比3驳回了特朗普的出生公民权行政令。首席大法官罗伯茨直接打脸。美国现在是什么状态？一个饭店老板，菜还没炒好就先掀桌子。自己人能打起来，这才叫真内耗。这个局面说明什么？说明我常说的"百年未有之大变局"正在加速。美国那套"组合拳"——关税战、科技战、金融战——打了快十年了，结果呢？中国不仅没倒，反而在高科技领域突飞猛进。再看耐克在中国销量跌了12%，中国品牌安踏、李宁在往上走。这就是工业化本体论的道理——你有工厂、有技术、有市场，你就能站住。美国现在很焦虑。核心问题就是我反复讲的——工业化空心化。B-52从1955年飞到今天还在飞，一摔死八个。沙特不听话。伊朗谈不拢。盟友各自打算盘。中国要做的就是把自己的事情做好。稳坐钓鱼台，看风云变幻。用实力赢得平等的时代已经到了。这就是战略相持的本质——谁先犯错，谁先扛不住。

宋鸿兵 — 美元环流正在逆转

如果你把今天全球金融市场的图景看作一个系统，它的核心问题只有一个：美元环流正在逆转。表面上看，标普500录得六年来最佳季度涨幅，AI热潮席卷一切。但系统的压力读数告诉我另外一回事。日元跌至162.69——这不是一个汇率数字，这是一个40年的压力阀门在崩裂。1986年至今，日元从未如此之弱。而日本的反应是什么？财务大臣表示"随时准备采取适当行动"。在152这个位置，日本过去两次都干预了。现在超了10个点还在忍？因为他们知道，干预是一把双刃剑。关键在于那条被市场系统性低估的传染路径：日元利差交易。借入廉价日元、买入纳斯达克科技股——这个套利电路已经运行了太久。一旦日本央行被迫收紧或财务省强力干预，利差交易平仓触发保证金追缴，连锁传导将从东京烧到纽约。这就像1883年胡雪岩垮台时一样——控制流动性的人，永远控制着资产价格的最终裁判权。黄金回到4000美元以下，看似下跌，实则从2024年2000美元翻倍而来——这与"风险偏好高涨"的市场叙事构成尖锐矛盾。聪明的钱正在两头下注：一边买AI股票，一边囤黄金。这叫系统冗余。美元环流的反向运动正在加速。沙特拒绝配合美军通过霍尔木兹海峡，这不是外交摩擦，这是石油美元体系的一个接点正在锈蚀。当一个盟友能对你说"不"的时候，金融高边疆的裂痕就已经出现了。所有信号指向同一个方向：这个系统的杠杆太高了，而一个单点事件——东京、德黑兰、或者某个保证金催缴——就足以让反馈回路反转。
