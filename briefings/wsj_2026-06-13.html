Top Story

SpaceX, Now Worth $2.1 Trillion, Pulls Off Goldilocks Debut

WSJ Print Edition · Front Page · Saturday, June 13, 2026

SpaceX marked the largest IPO in history on Friday, rallying 19.2% in its first day of trading on the Nasdaq. The rocket company opened at $150 per share after pricing at $135, closing at $160.95 — making Elon Musk the world's first trillionaire.

By the Numbers

  • IPO size: $75 billion raised — largest ever, 4x oversubscribed
  • Day 1 close: $160.95 (+19.2%) — market cap ~$2.1 trillion
  • Musk net worth: Surpassed $1 trillion milestone
  • Demand: Over $70 billion in retail orders
  • Index entry: Set to join Nasdaq-100 in 15 trading days

What Analysts Are Saying

  • CFRA: Issued a sell rating post-IPO, warning valuation may be stretched
  • Wolfe Research: "Out-of-this-world" valuation supported by rocket launch moat
  • Jay Woods (Freedom Capital): "Watch how we trade Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday"
  • Walter Isaacson: "Beginning of a whole new economy"
Broader Signal: The Magnificent Seven traded sideways as investors likely sold Big Tech to free up capital for SpaceX. OpenAI and Anthropic are expected to go public later this year — this IPO serves as a bellwether.

SpaceX's IPO Proves the Power of Musk's 'Superlative' Strategy

The rocket company might never accomplish all it has told investors, but it has met the original goal of reigniting interest in space. SpaceX's New Millionaires have the power to reshape the Texas real estate market as employees cash out stock options.

Economy & Policy

Inflation Hits 4% — First Time in Three Years

This week's consumer prices data showed annual inflation climbing above 4% for the first time in three years, adding to conviction that the Fed will be unable to cut rates anytime soon.

Fed Meeting Preview: Kevin Warsh's First as Chair

The Federal Reserve meets June 16-17 — the first helmed by Kevin Warsh. Expected to hold rates steady. Key watchpoints:

  • Any removal of easing language from the statement
  • Summary of Economic Projections — first under Warsh
  • Communication style: Warsh opposes press conferences every meeting

Odds of a rate cut this year: virtually zero (CME FedWatch).

Technology & AI

Anthropic Halts Access to Top AI Models After U.S. Ban on Foreign Use

Anthropic, valued at approximately $965 billion, has suspended access to its top-tier AI models following a U.S. government ban on foreign use of advanced AI systems. The move signals escalating AI export controls.

The AI Price War Is Here

Startups and tech giants alike are mixing and matching AI models to avoid premium prices charged by industry leaders like OpenAI and Anthropic. This "price war" is piling pressure on frontier labs as competition intensifies across the ecosystem.

The world's hunger for AI chips is straining the industry's ability to produce the materials they rely on, creating new pressure points that could slow the boom. Ajinomoto, a Japanese chemical company, produces critical film used in semiconductor manufacturing.
